This is the sample page for our submission 'Phase reconstruction in single channel speech enhancement based on phase gradients and estimated clean-speech amplitudes' to ICASSP 2024.

First, we present the synthetic phase retrived by the algorithm proposed in [1] from the estimated clean speech magnitude, in comparison with the noisy phase. It can be observed that the full synthetic phase sounds artificial.
Methods Noisy input Real CRUSE Real CRUSE-synthetic Real CRUSE-agnostic Clean ref

Now, we focus on the difference between the noisy phase and the estimated phase. Two baselines are provided here, real-CRUSE which predicts a real-valued magnitude mask from noisy magnitude, and complex-CRUSE, which predicts a complex mask from real- and imaginary noisy STFT coefficients. The performance upper bound, the signal composed of the estimated magnitude and the clean signal phase, is also provided for reference.
