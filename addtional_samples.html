We present here two sets of supplementary samples for our main demo page to show the generalisation ability of the proposed loss function component.
The first set is the same network trained on a more diverse dataset (~140 hours noisy speech from DNS challenge without reverberation) without hyperparamter re-finetuning. In general, the networks delivery better performance, and a similar trend among variants can be observed on those samples.
The second set demonstrates the improvement of the CEM loss on a faithful implementation of the CRUSE net [1], which is trained on a signal-based loss. The benefit of CEM loss on harmonic recovery can still be observed with a different loss function.

All samples have been rescaled to –26 dBov individually based on the active speech level (ASL).

The samples below are processed by:
  • Baseline 1: Basic: CRUSE net [1] predicting log-mask, all the following networks have the same architecture and training target;
  • Baseline 2: HAMSE: Trained with the loss function proposed in [2];
  • 2CEM: Trained with the proposed CEM loss on the spectral estimate, β=2;
  • adpCEM: Trained with the proposed CEM loss on the spectral estimate with an adaptive β;
  • maskCEM: Trained with the proposed CEM loss on the mask estimate, β=1.2;
